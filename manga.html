My top 5 Manga

1. Vagabond

2. The Climber

3. Real

4. Homunculus

5. Jojo's Bizarre Adventure

Vagabond

Vagabond is a critically acclaimed manga that tells a fictionalized story of the legendary swordsman Miyamoto Musashi. Written and illustrated by Takehiko Inoue, the series explores not only intense sword fights, but also deep philosophical themes such as self-discovery, discipline, and the meaning of strength. What makes *Vagabond* stand out is its stunning artwork. Every panel is carefully drawn, often resembling traditional Japanese ink paintings. The character development is another strong point — Musashi’s journey from a wild, violent youth into a thoughtful warrior is portrayed with great depth and realism. The pacing can be slow at times, especially compared to action-focused manga, but this actually adds to its reflective and mature tone. It is less about constant battles and more about inner growth and understanding life. Overall, *Vagabond* is considered a masterpiece in the manga world. Rating: 9.5/10

The Climber

The Climber is a psychological sports manga that follows the life of Buntaro Mori, a quiet and isolated high school student who discovers a passion for climbing. Created by Shinichi Sakamoto, the story is less about competition and more about the internal struggles of a person trying to find meaning through solitude and extreme challenges. What sets *The Climber* apart is its intense psychological depth. Mori is not a typical hero — he is distant, socially withdrawn, and often difficult to understand. His journey is raw and sometimes uncomfortable, but that’s what makes it feel real. The manga explores themes like loneliness, obsession, identity, and the human need to push beyond limits. The artwork is exceptional. Sakamoto’s detailed and realistic style perfectly captures both the beauty and danger of mountain climbing. The vertical panels and wide landscapes give a strong sense of height and isolation, making the reader almost feel the risk. The pacing can feel heavy and slow, especially during introspective moments, but it suits the tone of the story. This is not a light or easy read — it demands attention and emotional investment. Overall, *The Climber* is a powerful and unique manga that stands out for its realism and emotional intensity. Rating: 9/10

Real

Real is a deeply emotional sports manga written and illustrated by Takehiko Inoue. Unlike typical sports stories, it focuses on wheelchair basketball and the lives of people dealing with physical disabilities, trauma, and personal struggles. The story follows several characters, including Tomomi Nomiya, Kiyoharu Togawa, and Hisanobu Takahashi. Each of them faces different challenges — from guilt and regret to anger and loss — and the manga carefully shows how they cope, grow, and try to find purpose again. What makes *Real* exceptional is its realism and emotional weight. It doesn’t romanticize disability or struggle; instead, it presents them honestly and sometimes painfully. The character development is incredibly strong, with each person feeling complex and human. The artwork is expressive and detailed, capturing both physical movement and subtle emotions. Even without constant action, the story remains powerful through dialogue and internal reflection. The pacing is slow and thoughtful, which may not appeal to everyone, but it allows the themes to fully develop. Overall, *Real* is a mature and impactful manga that stands out for its honesty and depth. Rating: 9.3/10

Homunculus

Homunculus is a psychological horror manga written by Hideo Yamamoto. The story follows Susumu Nakoshi, a man living in his car who agrees to undergo a mysterious medical procedure called trepanation. After the experiment, he begins to see distorted manifestations of people’s inner selves, known as “homunculi.” What makes *Homunculus* stand out is its disturbing and deeply philosophical approach. The manga explores the human psyche, trauma, identity, and perception of reality. Each “homunculus” reflects hidden fears, desires, or psychological wounds, making the story both symbolic and unsettling. The artwork is realistic and often uncomfortable, perfectly matching the eerie tone of the narrative. Some scenes can be shocking or even disturbing, which may not be suitable for all readers, but they serve a purpose in emphasizing the psychological themes. The pacing is uneven at times, with long introspective sections, but this adds to the heavy, thought-provoking atmosphere. It’s not an easy read — it challenges the reader to interpret what is real and what is illusion. Overall, *Homunculus* is a unique and intense manga that dives deep into the darker side of the human mind. Rating: 9/10

Jojo's Bizarre Adventure

JoJo's Bizarre Adventure is a long-running and highly influential manga series created by Hirohiko Araki. The story is divided into multiple parts, each following a different member of the Joestar family, such as Jonathan Joestar and Jotaro Kujo, across different time periods and settings. What makes *JoJo’s Bizarre Adventure* unique is its creativity and style. The series is famous for its imaginative power system called Stands, introduced in later parts, where characters summon supernatural abilities with unique rules and strategies. Battles are less about strength and more about intelligence and clever use of abilities. Another standout aspect is the art style. Araki’s designs are bold, colorful, and inspired by fashion and classical art. The poses, expressions, and overall aesthetic give the manga a very distinct identity that sets it apart from others. The tone changes between parts — some are more serious, while others include humor and absurd moments — but this variety keeps the series fresh. However, the length and shifting style might not appeal to everyone. Overall, *JoJo’s Bizarre Adventure* is one of the most iconic manga series, known for its originality, memorable characters, and cultural impact. Rating: 9.2/10
